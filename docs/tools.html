Tools & Access

Which AI tools to use at DSPH, how to get them, and the data rules that come with each

This page is the reference for the tools we use across all three workshops. The short version: use Drexel-licensed tools for Drexel work, check the Drexel data classification for each tool, and never put regulated or sensitive data into a consumer tool.

Drexel’s official hub is AI at Drexel (Office of the Provost). The AI Tools page lists each tool’s availability and the data classification level it’s approved for—treat that page as the authoritative source if anything here and Drexel’s guidance differ.

ImportantThe One Rule to Remember

Regulated or sensitive information — PHI, non-directory PII, confidential HR information, protected student records, identifiable human-subjects data, contract-protected data, or export-controlled data — goes only into a tool and use case explicitly approved for it. When unsure, do not enter it. Use de-identified or synthetic data and ask for guidance.

ChatGPT Edu (Primary Tool)

ChatGPT Edu is Drexel’s institutionally supported version of ChatGPT. It is the default tool for most of what we do in these workshops.

What it gives you

  • Access to current OpenAI models in a Drexel-managed workspace. Use GPT-5.5 when it appears in your model picker; otherwise use the current default/latest GPT model available in the Drexel workspace.
  • Prompts and uploaded content are not used to train OpenAI’s models
  • Higher message limits than the free tier
  • The ability to build and share custom GPTs (reusable assistants with your own instructions and files) inside the Drexel workspace

How to get access

ChatGPT Edu is available now to Drexel faculty and professional staff (announcement). Request access and read the feature details through the IT service catalog: ChatGPT Edu Features and Access. Review the ChatGPT Terms of Use before you start. Bring any access questions to Workshop 1.

Data rules

Use is governed by the Drexel ChatGPT Edu Terms of Use. Drexel data is not used to train OpenAI models, but the Terms prohibit sensitive information that Drexel must protect by law, contract, or policy. Do not enter PHI, non-directory PII, confidential HR information, protected contracts, export-controlled data, or other restricted information. Student directory information may be used when not marked confidential, but use caution with student work and records. ChatGPT Edu does not override IRB protocols, data use agreements, HIPAA, FERPA, or University policy.

Microsoft Copilot

Drexel provides two Copilot experiences that matter for this training: Copilot Chat with Enterprise Data Protection and Microsoft 365 Copilot integrated into Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, and Teams. We will use both where available.

What it gives you

  • Web-based Copilot Chat for secure drafting, summarizing, and brainstorming when signed in with a Drexel account
  • Draft and rewrite in Word; summarize long documents
  • Summarize email threads and draft replies in Outlook
  • Generate formulas, summarize, and analyze in Excel
  • Recap meetings and action items in Teams

How to get access

Sign in with your Drexel account. For Copilot Chat, confirm that Enterprise Data Protection is active before using it with Drexel content. For Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, and Teams integration, confirm that you have the Microsoft 365 Copilot license enabled.

Data rules

Drexel’s AI Tools page lists Copilot Chat with Enterprise Data Protection as usable with High Risk Data when signed in with a Drexel account, and Low Risk Data otherwise. Microsoft 365 Copilot is listed as usable with High Risk Data for faculty and staff with the extra-cost license. Do not use a personal Microsoft account for Drexel work.

Zoom AI Companion (Meetings)

Zoom AI Companion is built into Drexel’s Zoom and is strongest for the work that happens in and around meetings.

What it gives you

  • Meeting summaries with key points and next steps, generated automatically
  • Smart recordings with highlights and chapters
  • Catch-up summaries if you join a meeting late
  • In-meeting questions about what’s been discussed so far

How to get access

It’s included with Drexel’s Zoom license at no extra cost. The meeting host turns it on for a given meeting; account and group settings (managed by Drexel IT) control availability. Participants are notified when it’s active.

Data rules

Zoom states it does not use your meeting audio, video, chat, or shared content to train Zoom’s or third-party AI models. Drexel lists Zoom AI Companion as usable with High Risk Data when a meeting is not recorded or transcribed, and Low Risk Data when it is recorded or transcribed. Notify participants when AI Companion is active and be careful with meetings that involve regulated or confidential information.

Adobe Creative Cloud Pro (Documents & Visuals)

Drexel provides Adobe tools with AI features. Acrobat AI Assistant and PDF Spaces are useful for document work; Creative Cloud and Express use Firefly for visuals.

What it gives you

  • Acrobat AI Assistant — summarize long PDFs, ask questions about them, and draft text (summaries, emails, key points) with citations back to the source passages.
  • Firefly / Adobe Express — generative image tools (text-to-image, generative fill and expand) and templates for flyers, infographics, social posts, and slide visuals. Drexel’s tool page notes monthly generative-credit limits. Adobe trains Firefly on licensed and public-domain content and positions output as commercially safe.

How to get access

Sign in with your Drexel account. Some Adobe licenses or premium features may involve extra cost.

Data rules

Drexel lists Adobe Acrobat AI Assistant, PDF Spaces, Creative Cloud, and Express as usable with Moderate Risk Data. Confirm the current classification on the AI Tools page before putting real documents into Acrobat AI Assistant. Review generated images before publishing—they can be inaccurate or misrepresent people and communities.

This tool is the focus of Workshop 4.

Consumer and PI-Purchased Tools

Claude, personal ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity, Consensus, OpenEvidence, Midjourney, and similar tools may be useful for public information, learning, and fully synthetic examples. They are not Drexel-supported tools unless separately approved through Drexel’s review process.

Use these tools with Low Risk Data only. Drexel notes that data shared with no-cost tools may be used for model training. If you want to purchase or use a tool at a higher data classification level, complete Drexel’s Third Party Risk Assessment process first.

TipQuick Self-Check Before You Paste

Ask yourself three questions: (1) Is this data regulated or covered by an agreement? (2) Is the tool I’m using approved for that data? (3) Could I use a de-identified or synthetic version instead? If any answer gives you pause, stop and check.
