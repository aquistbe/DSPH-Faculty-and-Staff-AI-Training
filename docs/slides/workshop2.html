SECTION 01

Where AI Fits

The biggest time savings come from the repetitive middle of a task.

WHICH TOOL, WHEN

Three Tools, Three Jobs

Microsoft 365 Copilot

Best when the work is already in a Microsoft file — a Word doc to rewrite, an Outlook thread to summarize, an Excel sheet to analyze.

Copilot Chat (EDP)

Useful for protected web-based drafting, summarizing, and brainstorming when you don’t need a reusable GPT.

ChatGPT Edu

Best for open-ended drafting, summarizing pasted text, file analysis, and reusable assistants. Use GPT-5.5 when available.

FIND YOUR CANDIDATES

What Makes a Good AI Task

AI handles the first draft, the summary, the reformat, the “make this clearer.” You bring the judgment and the final word.

  • Text-heavy — the work is mostly writing or reading.
  • Repeated — something you do over and over.
  • Low-stakes enough that a draft-plus-review is fine: status updates, recaps, first drafts.

SECTION 02

Ethics & Governance

Verification, disclosure, and over-reliance — what accountability means in daily work.

WORKFLOW ETHICS

Three Habits That Keep You Safe

Verify Before It Counts

AI can fabricate facts, stats, and citations. Check anything going into a real document or decision against the source.

Disclose Appropriately

Where AI informs a real decision or document, note that it was used and verify what matters.

Avoid Over-Reliance

Speed is the selling point and the trap. Keep your own judgment in the loop; don’t outsource the thinking.

SECTION 03

Hands-On

Use Copilot where your work lives, then run one real task in ChatGPT Edu.

ACTIVITY 1 · ~19 MIN

Microsoft 365 Copilot in Office

Track A — Word

Summarize a document into key points, rewrite a paragraph for a specific audience, and draft a new section from one line.

Track B — Outlook

Summarize a long thread and list decisions and action items, then draft a reply in a tone you specify.

Track C — Excel

Summarize what’s in the data, ask for a formula in plain language, and surface a trend or suggested chart.

ACTIVITY 2 · ~13 MIN

ChatGPT Edu for Your Real Task

Run one task you actually need this week, start to finish. Write a PTCF prompt, iterate 2–3 rounds, then do the final edit yourself.

  • Summarize-then-act — “Summarize this, then draft a 3-sentence email about what the team needs to do.”
  • Make-it-a-table — for options, pros and cons, or a schedule, add “Put this in a table.”
  • Build a reusable recipe — strip the specifics, leave placeholders, and save what worked.

What to Take Away
