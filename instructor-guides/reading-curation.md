# Reading Curation for DSPH AI Workshops

Review date: June 11, 2026

This folder has enough material for a full seminar, but the three DSPH workshops have only 3 contact hours. I would not assign required readings before the first series. Most participants will learn more from short, concrete practice with Drexel-approved tools. Use the readings as instructor preparation, optional references, and source material for future advanced sessions.

## Recommended Core References

These are the most useful pieces from `Readings/` for the current 3-workshop series.

| Reading | Best use | Why it helps |
|---|---|---|
| `Judgment is the skill that matters most in the AI era – Jim Grey on software man.pdf` | Workshop 1 framing | Clear argument that AI makes plausible output easy, while human judgment remains the scarce skill. Useful for the "AI drafts, you verify and decide" message. |
| `How People Are Really Using AI in 2026.pdf` | Workshop 1 or resources page | Broad, current examples of everyday AI use. Good for showing that adoption is practical and task-based, not only technical. |
| `Choosing to Stay Human - by Ethan Mollick.pdf` | Workshop 1 or optional follow-up | Helpful for discussing when to use AI, when not to use it, and how to keep human authorship and judgment visible. |
| `(1) How AI Is Changing Teaching Workflows.pdf` | Workshop 2, faculty examples | Good source for teaching and workflow examples. Use selectively because the audience includes staff as well as faculty. |
| `(1) AI-slop , GrantaGate and Bad Writing - by Tuhin Chakrabarty.pdf` | Workshop 2, writing quality | Good cautionary piece for weak AI prose. Use as instructor background or optional reading, not as a required reading. |
| `LLMs can unmask pseudonymous users at scale with surprising accuracy - Ars Techn.pdf` | Privacy reminder | Useful caution that de-identification and pseudonymity are not always enough. Good for reinforcing Drexel data-classification rules. |
| `Harvard College Adds Required AI Module to Expository Writing Curriculum - News .pdf` | Optional institutional comparison | Useful example of another university building basic AI literacy into instruction. |
| `Introducing workspace agents in ChatGPT - OpenAI.pdf` | Workshop 3 or future advanced session | Relevant for custom GPTs, shared tools, and workspace-level assistants. Use lightly in Workshop 3; save deeper discussion for advanced sessions. |

## Low Priority for This Series

Do not foreground the deep technical material in the first three workshops unless a participant asks. It will pull time away from practical use.

Examples: attention/QKV/token architecture pieces, GPU/data-center pieces, model-training papers, long agent-engineering guides, foundation-model benchmarking papers, and coding-agent setup notes.

These should be linked from the Resources page or mentioned during Workshop 3.

| Reference | Use |
